What is Engineering?

Merriam-Webster Dictionary defines engineering as "the application of science and mathematics by which the properties of matter and the sources of energy in nature are made useful to people." To put it simply, engineering is about solving problems in our world. To do so, engineers use math and science concepts to their advantage. Almost every man-made object was designed by an engineer. Everyday objects such as chairs, cars, personal computers, smartphones, and houses were all designed by engineers.

Engineering is a broad field, but it can been split into four main disciplines. These are: civil, chemical, mechanical, and electrical engineering (What is Engineering). Civil engineers focus on designing and maintaining both physical and natural structures. Bridges, skyscrapers, and roads are all designed by civil engineers. Chemical engineers focus more on materials and energy. Mechanical engineers deals mainly with machines. They would design cars, planes, or large industrial machines. Electrical engineers design things like circuits and computer networks. While these are the four main disciplines of engineering, there are plenty more to study. Other engineering disciplines borrow from a combination of the four main disciplines to create a unique field.

One problem engineering has always had is a lack of diversity. This is where UCLA's CEED program comes into play. The Center for Excellence in Engineering and Diversity (CEED) is a program that helps promote diversity within the field of engineering. Universities and companies alike are both recognizing this need for diversity and are taking action. Their efforts have been paying off, with data from Wired showing a gradual increase in the number of minorities studying computer science. The representation of minorities studying computer science has risen from 10% to 22% since 1992 (Myers, 2018).
